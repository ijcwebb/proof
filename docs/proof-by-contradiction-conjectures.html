Chapter 17 Proof by contradiction CONJECTURES

Exercise 17.1 Some of these conjectures are false; disprove them by finding a counterexample. Some of them are true; prove them using one (or a combination of more than one) of the methods we’ve met so far.

Conjecture 17.1 : \(\sqrt{3}\) is irrational.

Conjecture 17.2 : \(\pi\) is irrational.

Conjecture 17.3 : \(3a + 5b\) is even only when \(a\) and \(b\) are both integers.

Conjecture 17.4 : The product of three consecutive positive integers is never a perfect cube.

Conjecture 17.5 : The product of three consecutive integers is never a perfect cube (ignoring the trivial case where one of the three numbers is \(0\)).
Hint: Build on your work from Conjecture 17.4.

Conjecture 17.6 : In a right angled triangle,1 the length of the hypotenuse is always less than the sum of the lengths of the two other sides.

Conjecture 17.7 : Every perfect cube is either a multiple of \(9\), one more than a multiple of \(9\) or one less than a multiple of \(9\).

Conjecture 17.8 : The sum of two positive numbers is also positive.

Conjecture 17.9 : If \(a^2\) is an integer, then so is \(a\).

Conjecture 17.10 : If \(a\) is a perfect square, then \(a + 2\) isn’t.

Conjecture 17.11 : If \(a\) is a perfect square, then \(a^2\) isn’t.

Conjecture 17.12 : Every triangle has at least one obtuse angle.

Conjecture 17.13 : For any pair of integers \(a\) and \(b\), \((a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2\).

Conjecture 17.14 : For any pair of integers \(a\) and \(b\), \(\sqrt{a + b} = \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b}\).

Conjecture 17.15 : \(a^2 + a^3\) is even for any integer \(a\).

Conjecture 17.16 : For any integer \(a\), \(4 \nmid (a^2 - 3)\).

Conjecture 17.17 : There’s no positive integer \(a\) such that \(a^2 - 2a\) is negative.

Conjecture 17.18 : There’s no positive integer \(a\) such that \(a(a+1)\) is a perfect square.

Conjecture 17.19 : No circle has a circumference that’s equal to its area.

Conjecture 17.20 : No square has a perimeter that’s equal to its area.

Conjecture 17.21 : No rectangle has a perimeter that’s equal to its area.

Conjecture 17.22 : There are a finite amount of Pythagorean Triples.

  1. A right triangle is a triangle containing a \(90^\text{o}\) angle.↩︎
