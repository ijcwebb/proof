Chapter 16 Choosing the cases}

Once you have recognised that your proof will require cases (see Part~), it can sometimes be difficult deciding how to split the proof up.

The most important thing to remember when using cases is to make them (they should not overlap) and (they should consider all possible situations).

For example, if \(P\) says and you split the proof into positive integers and negative integers, then you have forgotten to consider \(a = 0\), and your cases are not complete. You will have to amend one of the two cases to include \(a = 0\), or add it as its own case. Similarly, if you have the cases \(a \geq 0\) and \(a \leq 0\), then 0 is contained in both cases, meaning the cases overlap, which also isn’t good. You should amend one of the cases so it no longer includes 0.
